Why RCTs

Most research measures what people think.
We measure what changes it.

A poll tells you 58% of voters support a policy, but it can't tell you which message drove them there, or whether a different message would have moved them more. That's the difference between correlation and causation. Traditional research describes what people already believe. It can't isolate what shifted those beliefs, because nothing was ever randomly assigned.

An RCT randomly assigns respondents to see a message (treatment group) or nothing (control group). The difference between the two groups is the causal effect of that message, isolated from everything else.

This is the same design used in clinical drug trials. It's the gold standard for proving causation in medicine, and it's the gold standard for proving causation in persuasion. When a treatment group moves 9 points more than a control group, you know the message did that, not ambient news, not the question order, not demographic drift.

What Makes It Different

Why RCTs beat every other
approach to message research.

Causal isolation

Unlike A/B testing on live campaigns or focus groups with self-selected participants, RCTs give you causal proof of what moved the needle. Random assignment eliminates confounding variables. You don't just know what performed, you know why.

Speed

Traditional research takes weeks and costs $50–100k per study. Grow Progress takes 24 hours at a fraction of the price. That's the difference between data you can act on today and data that arrives after the decision has already been made.

Subgroup precision

Every study breaks out results by key demographics, party, gender, age, geography, education, and more. You know not just what works overall, but who it works on. The message that moves suburban women may do nothing for rural independents.

FAQ

Common questions about
the methodology.

What's the minimum sample size?
Most studies use 1,000–2,000 respondents, split between treatment and control. Sample size is set to achieve 80%+ statistical power for detecting meaningful effect sizes. Grow Progress strategists help you size the study correctly based on your expected lift and number of variants.
How many message variants can I test?
2–8 variants in a single study. Each additional variant requires proportionally more respondents to maintain statistical power. Grow Progress will recommend the right number of variants based on your questions and budget.
How do you recruit respondents?
GP maintains a proprietary panel supplemented by trusted third-party providers. Panels are matched to your target audience, national, district-level, or custom. For political studies, samples can be matched to registered voter files for precise audience targeting.
What does the results report include?
Lift scores (treatment minus control), confidence intervals, statistical significance flags, subgroup breakdowns by party, gender, age, geography, and education, plus a written strategist recommendation. You get a clear narrative on what the findings mean for your next decision, not a raw data dump.
Can I test video ads?
Yes. Grow Progress supports text, image, video, and audio message formats. If your organization uses it to persuade people, it can be tested in a Grow Progress study.
