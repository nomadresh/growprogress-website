Member Mobilization · Healthcare Local

Dignity framing outperformed wages messaging across every member demographic

In a contract campaign for a major healthcare local, Grow Progress tested three message frames. The dignity and respect frame drove a 20-point lift in member mobilization intent, double the lift of the wages-and-benefits frame. The local used the findings to retrain 200 shop stewards before the authorization vote.

The outcome
The dignity frame outperformed wages messaging by 20 points in mobilization intent, delivering a clear playbook for steward training and member outreach ahead of the authorization vote.

Public Support · State Employees

Tested frames shifted public opinion on pension fights by 16 points

When a state employees union faced a hostile legislature, Grow Progress tested public-facing messages. A frame connecting pension security to teacher retention outperformed the standard "promise kept" frame by 16 points, shifting the public narrative before the legislative session.

The outcome
The teacher retention frame moved public support by 16 points, giving the union a tested message to lead with in press, digital, and grassroots outreach before the legislative fight began.

Strike Authorization · Healthcare Workers

Member solidarity messaging drove 22-point lift in strike authorization support

GP tested six message frames ahead of a high-stakes authorization vote. Solidarity framing, emphasizing that "when one of us is under attack, all of us are", produced the strongest lift. The local went on to win authorization with 87% member support.

The outcome
Solidarity framing drove a 22-point lift in authorization support. The local won the authorization vote with 87% member backing, equipped with tested language that unified the membership.

Ratification · Teachers Union

Contract ratification messaging drove 14-point improvement in member satisfaction

After a lengthy negotiation, Grow Progress tested how to frame the final contract to maximize member buy-in. Messages emphasizing long-term gains outperformed deficit-focused frames, helping the union ratify with strong approval despite a contentious bargaining year.

The outcome
Long-term gains framing improved member satisfaction scores by 14 points over the deficit frame, delivering a ratification playbook that secured strong approval after a difficult negotiation.
