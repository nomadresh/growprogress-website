What It Is

Segmentation research that tells you the 'why' behind the 'who.'

Audience Understanding Surveys are qualitative listening surveys that give you a rich, actionable creative brief that goes far beyond demographics. They let you identify people who are undecided or conflicted about your issue, cause, or candidate, and uncover what values, identities and world views drive them. Campaigns that develop messages from these insights are likely to outperform those that don't.

Choose the Right Tool

Audience Understanding vs.
Rapid Message Testing

Two different research questions. Two different products. Know which one you need.

Audience Understanding
Exploratory · Psychographic

"You don't yet know who to target or how they think."

Use AU when you need to:
  • Enter a new issue area without existing audience knowledge
  • Launch a new campaign and need to map the terrain first
  • Understand a hard-to-reach group, motivations, not just demographics
  • Build a message architecture from scratch, segments first, messages second
vs
Rapid Message Testing
Causal · A/B Tested

"You have messages and need to know which one performs."

Use RMT when you need to:
  • Pre-test creative before you spend at scale
  • Pick between 2–8 message variations with causal evidence
  • Get results in 24 hours to act before the news cycle changes
  • Prove to funders that creative decisions are grounded in data
What You Learn

The intelligence that makes every
message decision defensible.

AU research delivers four categories of insight no demographic breakdown can give you.

How different groups define the issue

Each segment comes to your issue with a different mental model. AU reveals what words, frames, and reference points each group uses, so you speak their language, not yours.

Where the persuadable middle lives

AU identifies the persuadable segments, and the precise conditions under which they shift, so you allocate effort where it counts.

What language to use, and avoid

Some words signal trust; others trigger skepticism. AU surfaces the specific vocabulary that lands with each group, and the landmines that sink otherwise strong messages.
