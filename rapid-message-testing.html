What It Is

A randomized controlled trial, built for persuasion.

RMT randomly assigns survey respondents to one of two groups. The treatment group sees your message, an ad script, a mail piece, a canvassing frame. The control group sees nothing.

The difference in opinion between the two groups is the causal effect of your message. Not correlation. Not self-reported preference. The actual persuasive impact of your content.

It's the same gold-standard methodology used in clinical drug trials and academic social science, adapted for the speed of modern communications. You don't need to guess which message works. You can know.

Why It Works

Built for the way
persuasion actually works.

Most research tells you what people think. RMT tells you what changes their minds.

Causal
Causal, not correlational
RCTs measure what changes minds, not just what people already believe. When random assignment creates the two groups, the only thing that can explain a difference in outcomes is the message itself.
Speed
Speed that matches your timeline
24-hour turnaround means you can test, learn, and adjust mid-campaign. Not six weeks later when the window has closed. Test before your next mail drop. Test before you finalize your ad buy.
Precision
Subgroup precision
See results broken down by party, demographics, and geography, not just topline. The message that moves suburban women may land differently with rural independents. Know before you deploy.
