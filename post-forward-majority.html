Forward Majority Helped Flip Four House Seats in Virginia With Grow Progress

Grow Progress
The Grow Progress Team
May 11, 2026·Client Spotlight
Virginia

On November 4th, 2025, Democrats swept four Virginia House of Delegates seats, flipping the chamber and defying expectations in a political environment that had felt increasingly uncertain. Behind that victory was Forward Majority, an organization that has built its reputation on rigorous, systematic testing of messages and strategies before committing resources to them. Their partnership with Grow Progress was central to that disciplined approach.

Key Takeaways
  • Iterate intentionally: Start with text, then move to video, then refinement for faster, smarter insights.
  • Look beyond the margins: Use deeper indicators to reveal genuine persuasion effects.
  • Challenge assumptions: Data-driven message testing leads to better outcomes and stronger movements.

A Smarter, More Disciplined Testing Framework

Forward Majority's approach to the 2025 Virginia cycle began well before election season. Rather than waiting until campaigns were underway to test messages, the organization built an off-cycle testing program designed to identify what worked before the pressure of a live race forced decisions.

Brian Herman, Forward Majority's Research Director, described the philosophy: "We believe that the best campaigns don't wing it on message. They earn the right to run their message by proving it works first. Grow Progress gave us the infrastructure to do that at scale and at speed."

The testing program was structured in deliberate phases. Forward Majority started with text-based message concepts, stripping out production value and creative execution to isolate the underlying argument. Only concepts that showed movement in text form advanced to video testing. This sequential approach eliminated weak frames early, before the team had invested in production.

From Text to Video: Refining What Works

Once text testing identified promising frames, Forward Majority moved to video, testing produced spots against each other with the same rigor applied to the text phase. The move from text to video introduced new variables, including messenger, tone, and visual framing, and not every frame that worked in text held up in video. The data drove those decisions too. Concepts that lost persuasive impact in translation were cut; concepts that gained impact with production were fast-tracked.

Making the Call When Results Are Close

One of the most valuable aspects of Forward Majority's testing discipline was how they handled close or ambiguous results. Rather than defaulting to gut instinct when margins were narrow, the team used secondary indicators, subgroup breakdowns, directional consistency across multiple waves, attitudinal shifts beyond the primary outcome, to understand what the data was actually saying.

"When the topline is close, that's when most campaigns make their worst decisions, they just pick what they already believed in. We used Grow Progress to go deeper: look at who was moving, what they were responding to, and why. Economic security and anxiety around cost of living were consistently driving the persuasion effects we were seeing, even when the headline numbers looked similar across messages."

What Sets Forward Majority Apart

Brian Herman identified three principles that he believes define Forward Majority's testing culture, and that he thinks are transferable to any organization serious about evidence-based campaigning:

  • Student's Mindset. "We come into every test genuinely not knowing the answer. The moment you start testing to confirm what you already believe, you've broken the methodology."
  • Resist Projecting Perspective. "The people we're trying to persuade don't think like campaign staffers. We test because we know our instincts are calibrated to a world that doesn't match the persuadable universe."
  • Language Matters. "Small word choices create meaningfully different responses. We've seen tests where two messages making the same argument, just worded differently, produce statistically different outcomes. You can't know that without testing."

Turning Insights Into Impact

The four flipped seats in Virginia were not an accident. They were the result of months of intentional work, testing before the campaign, refining during it, and trusting data over instinct when the two diverged. Forward Majority's partnership with Grow Progress made that discipline possible at the scale and speed a competitive cycle demands. As the 2026 cycle begins, the organization is already building the next round of tests, carrying forward a model that has now proven itself in one of the most consequential state legislative fights of the decade.
