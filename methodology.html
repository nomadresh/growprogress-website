How It Works

An experiment run at the
speed of a campaign.

01
Nationally representative panel
We recruit respondents matched to your target audience with demographic quotas — likely voters, parents, union members, whoever you need to reach.
02
Quality controls applied
Every respondent passes attention checks, speeder traps, and consistency filters before their response counts. Low-quality responses are replaced, not averaged in.
03
Random assignment
Half the sample is assigned to the control group, half to the test group — randomly. This is the foundation of causal inference. The two groups are statistically identical before your message appears.
04
One difference: your message
The test group sees your message. The control group sees neutral filler on an unrelated topic. Everything else — survey flow, question wording, response options — is identical.
05
Identical surveys for both groups
After exposure, both groups answer the same success question — typically support, favorability, or intended action. The control gives us the baseline. The test group shows the movement.
06
Results weighted to population
We apply raking weights to align the sample to real-world benchmarks — age, gender, race, education, geography — so your Lift score reflects the actual population you're trying to move.
07
Your Grow Progress Lift score — with confidence intervals
The weighted gap between test and control is your Lift. We report it with confidence intervals and significance markers so you always know how much to trust the result. A +8 point lift means 8 more people out of every 100 moved in the direction you intended — causally, not by coincidence.

08
Subgroup breakdowns included
Every study breaks down results by age, gender, race, education, party ID, and more — so you know not just whether a message works, but for whom.
Subgroup Analysis

The same message can persuade one group and backfire with another.

Overall lift tells you whether a message works on average. Subgroup analysis tells you for whom — and that's where campaigns and causes find their edge.

A job-creation frame may resonate with your supporters while alienating persuadables. Knowing the difference before you spend on media is what separates effective campaigns from expensive ones.
